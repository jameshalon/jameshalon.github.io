Live Streams

Starting May 16th 2022 my weekly live streams will be on a temporary hiatus. I am moving to a new town, which means I have to pack up and reconfigure my studio. I am targeting for mid-to-late summer 2022 for being back in action. In the meantime, you can watch and listen to all the previous streams on my YouTube channel or on Mixcloud.

I live stream DJ sets two times a week on Twitch.tv and Mixcloud. I have programmed each day to have a unique style, similar to how a club will book out. It's a chance for me to play different music I love and keeps it fresh for all of us. Every session also has paired VJ mixes using HalonVision, which is a custom built programmatically generative mixing system that creates a new and responsive experience every time.

If you miss a session, you can replay them at my YouTube channel or on Mixcloud.

  • Melodic Dreams Tuesday @ 5pm - 6pm Pacific
    Progressive & Melodic House. Lush soundscapes, relaxing vibes, and deep bass-lines.
  • Umbra Thursday @ 5pm - 6pm Pacific
    Driving, Underground & Dark Techno. Locked in at a 130bpm.

Biography

contact & booking: contact @ jameshalon.com

promos: promos @ jameshalon.com

DJ and producer of House, Melodic House, Progressive House & Techno.

Based in San Diego CA, past residences in San Francisco CA and Dallas TX.

Starting out under the moniker DJ3 circa 1996, James Halon was a resident at Record Resource and played events in Denton and Dallas TX. Moving to San Francisco in 1999, James started weekly events with For The Music & helped found the music side of False Profit.

James ran the sound system for False Profit at 43 Norfolk and put on many amazing nights at the warehouse. He has also joined forces and played with many crews and venues, including Dub Beautiful, Ambient Airlines, Space Cowboys, B-Family, Eklektic, Future Breaks, Tenderloft, Tags Trance Trip, GrooveTech Radio, Anon Salon, Trust, Wish, Mighty SF, DNA Lounge, 1015 Folsom, and many more.

2004, James co-founded Cedub Studios and moved away from DJing to focus on production and engineering for local artists. During this time he helped create and launch Fake Science as a podcast, then expanded to online digital music distribution and A&R. He produced tracks under the Cell Culture act with Chris Walcott. He also produced tracks as The Clockmakers with DJ Yap, under the moniker DJ Bespoke.

In 2009, James moved from the Bay and headed to his new home, San Diego. After taking a longer than expected break to settle into his new home, James is building out a studio and has returned to producing and DJing. Starting in 2020, James began weekly live streams, featuring new, classic and original tracks focusing on all things House x Techno.
